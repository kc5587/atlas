Honest Signal Detection Across the AI Value Chain

Lead–lag structure, variance risk premia, and the discipline of the null result
K. Chitturi · Independent research · 29 firms · 9 stages · 13 pre-registered hypotheses
Abstract

We test whether economically-motivated signals propagate across the artificial-intelligence supply chain, or are arbitraged away. Using point-in-time SEC fundamentals, daily returns, and options-implied volatility, each hypothesis is evaluated with a selection-aware bootstrap null, Benjamini–Hochberg control across declared families, and out-of-sample validation. Of thirteen hypotheses, four are confirmed, one is suggestive, and eight are null. The pattern is consistent with efficient pricing of slow fundamental signals — with two exceptions: genuine capex→revenue propagation, and a compensated volatility risk premium. The nulls are not failures; they are the result.

1The value chain

The object of study is a directed graph of supplier relationships among the firms that build and operate AI infrastructure, from electronic-design-automation tools through foundries and chips to the hyperscale cloud.1 Edges encode a supplies relation; the question throughout is whether a shock at one node is informative about a later move at a node downstream of it.

1.Nodes carry a CIK where a US filer exists; ASML and TSM are included structurally but excluded from fundamental tests (no SEC filings). Stage membership is fixed ex-ante and never tuned to a result.
